Muhammad Ali Jinnah

The founding father of Pakistan, Jinnah played a pivotal role in the creation of the nation and championed the cause of a separate state for Muslims in British India.

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali Jinnah life:

  • 1876 - Born on December 25 in Karachi, British India (now Pakistan)
  • 1893 - Completes his education in London, studying at the Inns of Court School of Law
  • 1896 - Begins his legal practice in Bombay (now Mumbai)
  • 1913 - Joins the All-India Muslim League
  • 1916 - Becomes a member of the Imperial Legislative Council
  • 1917 - Plays a crucial role in the Lucknow Session of the Muslim League, promoting Hindu-Muslim unity
  • 1920 - Elected as the President of the All-India Home Rule League
  • 1927 - Serves as the leader of the Muslims at the All-Party Conference
  • 1934 - Resigns from the All-India Legislative Assembly
  • 1940 - Presents the Lahore Resolution on March 23, advocating for the creation of an independent Muslim state
  • 1947 - Successfully negotiates for the creation of Pakistan and becomes its first Governor-General
  • 1948 - Muhammad Ali Jinnah passes away on September 11 in Karachi
